The most expensive MBBS-abroad mistake is choosing a university first and asking FMGE readiness questions later.

## What FMGE awareness actually means
FMGE awareness is not obsessing over one percentage. It means understanding exam pattern alignment, language of instruction, clinical exposure quality, and support you need during and after your degree.

## Start with regulatory fit, not brochure rankings
Check NMC-aligned requirements, internship pathways, and documentation standards before discussing campus photos. A university that looks premium but creates recognition friction can delay your career path.
> Insight Block: Families compare tuition but skip academic recovery systems; recovery systems matter more when a student falls behind.

## Evaluate curriculum and exam compatibility
Review whether core subjects, teaching cadence, and assessment style map to FMGE/NExT preparation rhythm. Students often struggle where foundational sciences are rushed.

## Ask hard questions about student support
Who mentors Indian students? Is there an intervention plan for low performers? Is there structured revision support in final years? A vague "we help everyone" is not support architecture.

## Red flags families should catch early
Unverified guaranteed claims, unclear alumni pathways, and pressure to pay quickly are warning signs. Good consultants educate before they sell and document every step in writing.
> Insight Block: The right consultant reduces uncertainty by documentation discipline, not loud promises.

## External references to verify claims
Use official NMC notices, National Board exam updates, and country education ministry sources when validating claims.

## Actionable close for students and parents
Build a comparison sheet with five universities and score compliance clarity, academic support, and FMGE/NExT readiness signals.
