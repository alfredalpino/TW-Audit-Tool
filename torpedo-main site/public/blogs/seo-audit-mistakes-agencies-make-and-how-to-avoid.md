If your SEO audit produces a 60-slide deck but no movement in qualified leads after 30 days, the audit failed in priorities.

## Why most audits underperform in real businesses
Many agencies optimize for audit theatre: long issue lists and generic recommendations. Founders need one question: which fix changes demand capture or conversion this quarter? Build your audit around business intent, not crawler curiosity.

## Mistake #1: treating all errors as equal
A broken canonical cluster on money pages matters more than five alt tags on old blog images. Score each finding by impact, confidence, and effort. That score forces strategic sequencing.
> Insight Block: The highest-ROI SEO audits are not the most comprehensive; they reduce decision latency between diagnosis and deployment.

## Mistake #2: ignoring search intent mismatch
Pages can rank and still fail. If a service page targets informational intent while sales expects demos, conversion stays weak. Audit SERP intent first and align the page format accordingly.

## Mistake #3: divorcing technical findings from analytics
SEO and CRO cannot be separate tracks. Cross-check top landing pages in GA4 and Search Console. If pages with strong impressions have weak form completion, your issue may be UX friction.

## A practical 14-day correction workflow
Day 1-2: define revenue pages. Day 3-4: indexability and crawl checks. Day 5-7: intent and content gaps. Day 8-10: internal linking and schema. Day 11-14: publish fixes and instrument outcomes.
> Insight Block: Ranking gains without offer-page clarity often increase low-intent inquiries.

## Actionable close for founders and marketing leads
Ask your agency for a top-10 prioritized fix list with expected effect, owner, and due date. If they cannot produce it quickly, the audit is still too abstract.
