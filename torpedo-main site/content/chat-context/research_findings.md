# TorpedoWeb LLC - Legal & Compliance Research Findings

## Delaware LLC Law (Title 6, Chapter 18)
- **Operating Agreement (§ 18-101):** Legally defined as a "Limited Liability Company Agreement." Can be written, oral, or implied. Highly recommended to be written for professional agencies.
- **Formation (§ 18-201):** Requires filing a Certificate of Formation with the Secretary of State.
- **Registered Agent (§ 18-104):** Must maintain a registered agent and office in Delaware.
- **Contractual Freedom (§ 18-1101):** Delaware law prioritizes the "freedom of contract," allowing LLCs to define their own governance and liability structures extensively in the Operating Agreement.
- **Blockchain/Distributed Ledger (§ 18-113):** Delaware explicitly allows the use of electronic transmissions and distributed ledgers for maintaining records.

## Federal & International Compliance
- **Remote Work:** Compliance is generally governed by the location of the employee/contractor, not the company's registration.
- **Tax (IRS):** Requires Form W-9 for U.S. contractors and W-8BEN/W-8BEN-E for international contractors.
- **Data Protection:** GDPR (EU), CCPA (California), and other regional laws apply based on client/user location.

## Top 10 Critical Documents Identified
1. **LLC Operating Agreement:** Internal governance and liability protection. (Legally Required/Best Practice)
2. **Master Services Agreement (MSA):** Core client relationship framework. (Industry Best Practice)
3. **Statement of Work (SOW):** Project-specific deliverables and timelines. (Industry Best Practice)
4. **Independent Contractor Agreement (ICA):** For remote international/U.S. team members. (Industry Best Practice)
5. **Intellectual Property (IP) Assignment Agreement:** Ensures company owns code/assets. (Critical Risk Mitigation)
6. **Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA):** Protection of trade secrets and client data. (Professional Standard)
7. **Privacy Policy & Data Processing Addendum (DPA):** Compliance with GDPR/CCPA. (Legally Required for many clients)
8. **Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) / Terms of Service:** For any hosted systems or platforms. (Professional Standard)
9. **Change Order Form:** Formalizes scope changes to prevent disputes. (Operational Protection)

## Risk Mitigation Focus
- **IP Ownership:** "Work made for hire" vs. explicit assignment.
- **Limitation of Liability:** Capping damages at fees paid.
- **Indemnification:** Protecting against third-party claims.
- **Governing Law:** Delaware (for consistency).
- **Dispute Resolution:** Mandatory arbitration (JAMS/AAA).

## Remote Contractor Compliance
- **IP Assignment (International):** While Delaware law is permissive, local laws of the contractor (e.g., India, UK, Brazil) may require specific "moral rights" waivers or separate local assignment agreements to be fully enforceable.
- **Tax Withholding:** U.S. companies must collect Form W-8BEN (individuals) or W-8BEN-E (entities) from international contractors to certify their non-U.S. status and avoid 30% withholding requirements.
- **Classification Risk:** Agreements must clearly define the relationship as an "Independent Contractor" to avoid unintentional employment status, focusing on the contractor's control over the "manner and means" of work.

## Professional Document Standards
- **Master Services Agreement (MSA):** Should include "Broad Transfer of IP Ownership" upon full payment, "Limitation of Liability" (Dollar Cap), and "IP Indemnity" with specific exclusions for combination claims.
- **Change Orders:** Essential for "Agile" development to prevent scope creep and payment disputes.
- **Acceptance Procedures:** Defined timelines (e.g., 5-10 business days) for client review of deliverables to prevent indefinite project stalls.

## Professional Best Practices & Industry Standards
- **Two-Tier Agreement Structure:** Top-tier agencies use a Master Services Agreement (MSA) for overarching legal terms and separate Statements of Work (SOW) for project-specific details. This allows for rapid scaling and repeated projects without re-negotiating core legalities.
- **Client Onboarding Handbook:** Beyond legal documents, professional agencies provide a "Welcome Guide" or "Onboarding Handbook" that details communication protocols (Slack/Email), project management tools (Jira/Linear), and escalation paths. This builds professional credibility and sets expectations.
- **Information Security Policy (ISP):** For agencies handling custom infrastructure, a SOC 2-aligned ISP is a "Professional Enhancement" that elevates brand authority. It should cover data encryption, access control (MFA), and incident response.
- **Code of Conduct:** A public or internal Code of Conduct establishes ethical standards for both the team and clients, covering harassment, professional integrity, and conflicts of interest.
- **Disaster Recovery & Business Continuity:** Essential for remote agencies. Documents should outline how the agency maintains service if key infrastructure (GitHub, AWS) or team members are unavailable.
