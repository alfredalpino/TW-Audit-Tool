# TorpedoWeb LLC Operating Agreement

This Operating Agreement (this "Agreement"), dated as of [Date], is entered into by and among the undersigned members (the "Members") of TorpedoWeb LLC, a Delaware limited liability company (the "Company").

## RECITALS

A. The Company was formed as a limited liability company under the provisions of the Delaware Limited Liability Company Act, 6 Del. C. § 18-101 et seq. (the "Act"), by the filing of a Certificate of Formation with the Secretary of State of the State of Delaware on [Date of Formation].

B. The Members desire to enter into this Agreement to set forth their respective rights, duties, and obligations with respect to the Company and to provide for the regulation and management of the affairs of the Company.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Members agree as follows:

## ARTICLE I: DEFINITIONS

1.1 **Act:** The Delaware Limited Liability Company Act, as amended from time to time.
1.2 **Agreement:** This Operating Agreement, as amended from time to time.
1.3 **Capital Account:** An account maintained for each Member in accordance with Treasury Regulation § 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv).
1.4 **Capital Contribution:** Any cash, property, services rendered, or promissory note or other obligation to contribute cash or property or to perform services, which a Member contributes to the Company in the Member’s capacity as a Member.
1.5 **Certificate of Formation:** The Certificate of Formation of the Company filed with the Secretary of State of the State of Delaware, as amended from time to time.
1.6 **Company:** TorpedoWeb LLC, a Delaware limited liability company.
1.7 **Confidential Information:** Any information, including but not limited to, trade secrets, business plans, financial data, client lists, technical data, software code, and marketing strategies, whether oral, written, or electronic, that is not generally known to the public and is disclosed to a Member by the Company or another Member.
1.8 **Chief of Staff:** The person designated as Chief of Staff of the Company, as set forth in Article V. Authority is limited to staff management as set forth in Article V.
1.9 **CFO:** The Chief Financial Officer of the Company, as set forth in Article V. Authority is limited to financial matters as set forth in Article V.
1.10 **COO:** The Chief Operating Officer of the Company, as set forth in Article V. Authority is limited to operations as set forth in Article V.
1.11 **Director:** The person designated as Director (Founder/Director) of the Company, as set forth in Article V. Sole authority to determine Distributable Cash and the timing of distributions thereof is vested in the Director. The CEO reports to the Director.
1.12 **Distributable Cash:** All cash and cash equivalents of the Company on hand from time to time, *minus* (a) amounts reserved by the Director for the Company’s reasonable business needs, including working capital, payment of debts and obligations, capital expenditures, reserves for contingencies or liabilities, and compliance with any financing or other agreements, and (b) any amounts that the Director determines should be retained for the conduct of the Company’s business. Distributable Cash does not include cash that is restricted by contract or law or that the Director in good faith deems necessary to retain. The amount of Distributable Cash and the timing of any determination thereof shall be determined solely by the Director in the Director’s reasonable discretion.
1.13 **CEO:** The Chief Executive Officer of the Company, as set forth in Article V. The CEO reports to the Director and has authority over the Chief of Staff, CFO, and COO.
1.14 **Manager(s):** Collectively, the CEO, Chief of Staff, CFO, and COO, each with authority limited as set forth in Article V.
1.15 **Member:** Any person executing this Agreement as a Member, and any other person who may hereafter be admitted as a Member of the Company as provided herein.
1.16 **Membership Interest:** A Member’s entire interest in the Company, including: (a) the Member’s share of the profits and losses of the Company; (b) the Member’s right to receive distributions of the Company’s assets (including Distributable Cash); and (c) the Member’s voting rights with respect to Company matters, which shall be in proportion to such Member’s Membership Interest as set forth in Schedule A and as otherwise provided in this Agreement.

## ARTICLE II: FORMATION AND NAME

2.1 **Formation:** The Company was formed as a Delaware limited liability company on [Date of Formation] by the filing of the Certificate of Formation with the Secretary of State of the State of Delaware.
2.2 **Name:** The name of the Company is TorpedoWeb LLC.
2.3 **Registered Office and Agent:** The registered office of the Company in the State of Delaware shall be [Registered Office Address], and the name of its registered agent at such address shall be [Registered Agent Name].
2.4 **Purpose:** The purpose of the Company is to engage in any lawful act or activity for which limited liability companies may be formed under the Act, including but not limited to, providing digital engineering services, custom web development, infrastructure builds, backend systems, and automation workflows.
2.5 **Term:** The term of the Company shall be perpetual unless sooner dissolved in accordance with the provisions of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE III: CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS AND CAPITAL ACCOUNTS

3.1 **Initial Capital Contributions:** Each Member shall make an initial Capital Contribution to the Company as set forth in Schedule A attached hereto.
3.2 **Additional Capital Contributions:** No Member shall be required to make any additional Capital Contributions without the express written consent of all Members.
3.3 **Capital Accounts:** A Capital Account shall be maintained for each Member in accordance with Treasury Regulation § 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv).

## ARTICLE IV: ALLOCATIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS

4.1 **Allocations of Profits and Losses:** The Company’s profits and losses shall be allocated among the Members in proportion to their respective Membership Interests, as set forth in Schedule A.
4.2 **Distributions of Distributable Cash:** Distributable Cash shall be distributed to the Members in proportion to their respective Membership Interests, as set forth in Schedule A, at such times as determined solely by the Director.

## ARTICLE V: MANAGEMENT

**5.1 Management Hierarchy.** The Company’s management is structured as follows. The Founder/Director is the highest authority. The CEO reports to the Founder/Director. The Chief of Staff, CFO, and COO report to the CEO. The CEO is the manager of all other managers (Chief of Staff, CFO, and COO). Each manager’s authority is limited to the scope set forth in this Article V, and each must obtain and take the final decision from the person to whom they report before taking any act within their authority.

**5.2 Founder/Director.** The Company shall have a Director (the “Founder/Director”). The initial Director shall be Ubaid Ur Rahman Siddiqui. The Director may be the same person as a Member. Removal and replacement of the Director shall be by the affirmative vote of Members holding a majority of the Membership Interests. The Director has (a) sole authority to determine the amount of Distributable Cash and the timing of any distribution of Distributable Cash to Members—no other manager has any authority over such determinations—and (b) final decision-making authority over the Company; the CEO shall obtain and take the final decision from the Director before taking any material act. The CEO is under the Director.

**5.3 CEO (Chief Executive Officer).** The Company shall have a CEO. The initial CEO shall be [Name of CEO]. The CEO reports to the Founder/Director and is the manager of all other managers (Chief of Staff, CFO, and COO). The CEO has authority over the overall management and strategic direction of the Company, subject at all times to obtaining the Director’s final decision where required under this Agreement. The CEO shall obtain and take the final decision from the Director before taking any material act. The Chief of Staff, CFO, and COO shall obtain and take the final decision from the CEO (unless this Agreement or the Director requires that a matter be referred to the Director) before taking any act within their respective authorities. Removal and replacement of the CEO shall be by the Director or by the affirmative vote of Members holding a majority of the Membership Interests.

**5.4 Chief of Staff.** The Company may have a Chief of Staff. The initial Chief of Staff shall be [Name of Chief of Staff]. The Chief of Staff reports to the CEO. The Chief of Staff’s authority is **limited to staff management only**, including: (a) hiring, termination, and discipline of employees and independent contractors (other than the CEO, CFO, and COO); (b) internal coordination and allocation of staff; (c) personnel policies and workplace matters; and (d) such other staff-related matters as the CEO may delegate. The Chief of Staff shall have **no** authority over financial decisions (reserved to the CFO and Director), operations (reserved to the COO), Distributable Cash (reserved to the Director), or strategic or company-wide decisions (reserved to the CEO and Director). The Chief of Staff shall obtain and take the final decision from the CEO before taking any act within the Chief of Staff’s authority. Removal and replacement of the Chief of Staff shall be by the CEO or by the Director.

**5.5 CFO (Chief Financial Officer).** The Company shall have a CFO. The initial CFO shall be [Name of CFO]. The CFO reports to the CEO. The CFO’s authority is **limited to financial matters only**, including: (a) maintaining books and records and financial reporting; (b) budgets and financial planning; (c) banking, treasury, and cash management (subject to the Director’s sole authority over Distributable Cash and the timing of distributions); (d) tax, insurance, and compliance with financial obligations; and (e) such other financial matters as the CEO may delegate. The CFO shall have **no** authority over staff (reserved to the Chief of Staff), operations (reserved to the COO), the amount or timing of Distributable Cash distributions (reserved to the Director), or strategic or company-wide decisions (reserved to the CEO and Director). The CFO shall obtain and take the final decision from the CEO before taking any act within the CFO’s authority. Removal and replacement of the CFO shall be by the CEO or by the Director.

**5.6 COO (Chief Operating Officer).** The Company shall have a COO. The initial COO shall be [Name of COO]. The COO reports to the CEO. The COO’s authority is **limited to operations only**, including: (a) day-to-day operations and execution of the Company’s business; (b) processes, delivery, and project execution; (c) operational policies and procedures; and (d) such other operational matters as the CEO may delegate. The COO shall have **no** authority over staff (reserved to the Chief of Staff), financial decisions or Distributable Cash (reserved to the CFO and Director), or strategic or company-wide decisions (reserved to the CEO and Director). The COO shall obtain and take the final decision from the CEO before taking any act within the COO’s authority. Removal and replacement of the COO shall be by the CEO or by the Director.

**5.7 Removal and Replacement of Managers Generally.** Except as set forth in Sections 5.2, 5.3, 5.4, 5.5, and 5.6, a Manager may be removed, with or without cause, by the affirmative vote of Members holding a majority of the Membership Interests. A vacancy in any manager position shall be filled as set forth in this Article V or by the affirmative vote of Members holding a majority of the Membership Interests.

## ARTICLE V-A: LIMITATION OF DUTIES; EXCULPATION; ADVANCEMENT

5A.1 **Limitation of Fiduciary Duties.** To the fullest extent permitted by the Delaware Limited Liability Company Act, 6 Del. C. § 18-1101(c), and any other applicable law, the doctrine of fiduciary duty shall not apply to the Manager(s) or Members. The duties of the Manager(s) and Members are limited to the contractual duties set forth in this Agreement. To the extent that any fiduciary duty is deemed to apply, such duty is hereby eliminated to the maximum extent permitted by 6 Del. C. § 18-1101(c).
5A.2 **Exculpation.** No Manager or Member shall be liable to the Company or any Member for any claim arising out of or in connection with the business or affairs of the Company, except for (a) acts or omissions involving bad faith, willful misconduct, or knowing violation of law, or (b) any transaction from which the Manager or Member derived an improper personal benefit. This exculpation is in addition to any limitation of liability permitted under the Act.
5A.3 **Advancement of Expenses.** The Company may, in the discretion of the Manager(s), advance reasonable expenses (including attorneys' fees) incurred by a Manager or Member in defending any proceeding in advance of final disposition, provided that the recipient furnishes an undertaking to repay if it is ultimately determined that the recipient is not entitled to indemnification. The right to advancement is contractual and not exclusive of any other right under the Act or this Agreement.
5A.4 **No Duty to Present Opportunities.** Neither the Manager(s) nor any Member shall have any duty to offer to the Company any business opportunity of which they become aware, and each may pursue such opportunities for their own account or for the account of others, unless such opportunity was developed using Company resources or Confidential Information in breach of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE VI: MEMBERS

6.1 **Limited Liability:** The liability of each Member for the debts, obligations, and liabilities of the Company shall be limited to the amount of their Capital Contribution, as provided in the Act.
6.2 **Voting Rights:** Except as otherwise provided in this Agreement, Members shall have voting rights in proportion to their respective Membership Interests.
6.3 **Meetings of Members:** Meetings of the Members may be called by any Manager or by Members holding at least [Percentage]% of the Membership Interests. Notice of any meeting shall be given at least [Number] days prior to the meeting.
6.4 **Action Without Meeting:** Any action required or permitted to be taken at a meeting of the Members may be taken without a meeting if a consent in writing, setting forth the action so taken, is signed by Members holding the minimum number of Membership Interests that would be necessary to authorize or take such action at a meeting at which all Members entitled to vote thereon were present and voted.

## ARTICLE VII: TRANSFERS OF MEMBERSHIP INTERESTS

7.1 **Restrictions on Transfer:** No Member shall transfer, assign, encumber, or otherwise dispose of all or any part of their Membership Interest without the prior written consent of the Manager(s) and Members holding a majority of the remaining Membership Interests.
7.2 **Right of First Refusal:** Before any Member may transfer their Membership Interest to a third party, the transferring Member must first offer to sell such Membership Interest to the Company and the other Members at the same price and on the same terms as offered to the third party.

## ARTICLE VIII: DISSOLUTION AND LIQUIDATION

8.1 **Events of Dissolution:** The Company shall be dissolved upon the occurrence of any of the following events:
    a. The unanimous written consent of all Members;
    b. The entry of a decree of judicial dissolution under the Act;
    c. The sale or other disposition of all or substantially all of the assets of the Company.
8.2 **Liquidation:** Upon dissolution, the Manager(s) shall wind up the affairs of the Company and liquidate its assets. The proceeds of liquidation shall be applied in the following order:
    a. To the payment of debts and liabilities of the Company;
    b. To the establishment of reserves for contingent liabilities;
    c. To the Members in proportion to their positive Capital Account balances.

## ARTICLE IX: INDEMNIFICATION

9.1 **Indemnification of Manager(s) and Members:** The Company shall indemnify and hold harmless each Manager and Member to the fullest extent permitted by the Act from and against any and all losses, claims, damages, liabilities, expenses (including reasonable attorneys’ fees and expenses), judgments, fines, and amounts paid in settlement, incurred by such Manager or Member in connection with the business and affairs of the Company, provided that such Manager or Member acted in good faith and in a manner reasonably believed to be in the best interests of the Company.

## ARTICLE X: CONFIDENTIALITY

10.1 **Confidentiality Obligations:** Each Member acknowledges that they will have access to Confidential Information of the Company. Each Member agrees to keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential and not to disclose or use such Confidential Information for any purpose other than for the benefit of the Company.

## ARTICLE XI: GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

11.1 **Governing Law:** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflicts of laws principles.
11.2 **Arbitration:** THE PARTIES WAIVE ANY RIGHT TO A JURY TRIAL AND AGREE THAT ANY PROCEEDING SHALL BE CONDUCTED ON AN INDIVIDUAL BASIS AND NOT IN A CLASS, CONSOLIDATED, OR REPRESENTATIVE ACTION. Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement, or the breach, termination, or invalidity thereof, shall be settled by binding arbitration in accordance with the Commercial Arbitration Rules of the American Arbitration Association (AAA). The place of arbitration shall be Wilmington, Delaware. The arbitration shall be conducted by a single arbitrator. The arbitration and all related proceedings shall be kept confidential by the parties and the arbitrator except as required by law or to enforce an award. The award rendered by the arbitrator shall be final and binding upon the parties, and judgment thereon may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction in the State of Delaware. The parties consent to exclusive jurisdiction and venue in the state and federal courts located in Wilmington, Delaware for any action to enforce an award or for any proceeding not subject to arbitration.

## ARTICLE XII: MISCELLANEOUS

12.1 **Entire Agreement:** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement among the Members with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings, both written and oral.
12.2 **Amendments:** This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument executed by all Members.
12.3 **Severability:** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.
12.4 **Counterparts:** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, but all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument.
12.5 **Electronic Signatures:** This Agreement may be executed by electronic signature, which shall be considered as an original signature for all purposes.
