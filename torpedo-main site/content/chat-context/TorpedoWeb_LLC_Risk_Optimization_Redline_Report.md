## 1. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY (LOL)

### 1.1 Master Services Agreement (Article VII)

**Current (7.1–7.2):** Consequential-damages exclusion and single aggregate cap with carve-outs for indemnity, confidentiality, and gross negligence/willful misconduct.

**Redlined replacement:**

**7.1 Exclusion of Consequential and Specified Damages.** TO THE MAXIMUM EXTENT PERMITTED BY APPLICABLE LAW, IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, PUNITIVE, OR EXEMPLARY DAMAGES, OR FOR LOSS OF PROFITS, REVENUE, BUSINESS OPPORTUNITY, GOODWILL, ANTICIPATED SAVINGS, LOSS OR CORRUPTION OF DATA, OR OTHER INTANGIBLE LOSSES, WHETHER BASED IN CONTRACT, TORT (INCLUDING NEGLIGENCE), STRICT LIABILITY, OR OTHERWISE, ARISING OUT OF OR IN CONNECTION WITH THIS AGREEMENT OR ANY SOW, EVEN IF SUCH PARTY HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

**7.2 Cap on Liability.** EXCEPT FOR (A) A PARTY'S INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS UNDER ARTICLE VIII, (B) BREACH OF ARTICLE V (CONFIDENTIALITY), AND (C) LIABILITY ARISING FROM SUCH PARTY'S GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT, EACH PARTY'S LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT AND ALL RELATED SOWs SHALL BE LIMITED AS FOLLOWS: (I) WITH RESPECT TO ANY SINGLE CLAIM OR SERIES OF RELATED CLAIMS ARISING FROM THE SAME FACTS OR CIRCUMSTANCES, LIABILITY SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID BY CLIENT TO SERVICE PROVIDER UNDER THE APPLICABLE SOW(s) DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM; AND (II) IN THE AGGREGATE FOR ALL CLAIMS UNDER THIS AGREEMENT AND ALL SOWs DURING THE TERM, LIABILITY SHALL NOT EXCEED ONE AND ONE-HALF (1.5) TIMES THE TOTAL FEES PAID BY CLIENT TO SERVICE PROVIDER IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE FIRST EVENT GIVING RISE TO LIABILITY. THESE LIMITATIONS APPLY WHETHER THE CLAIM IS BASED IN CONTRACT, TORT (INCLUDING NEGLIGENCE), STRICT LIABILITY, OR OTHERWISE.

**Rationale:** (1) Express exclusion of lost profits, revenue, business opportunity, goodwill, anticipated savings, and data removes ambiguity and supports enforceability. (2) Explicit application to contract, tort, negligence, and strict liability avoids tort-based end-runs. (3) Per-claim cap plus aggregate cap (1.5x 12-month fees) bounds exposure across related claims. (4) Narrow carve-outs (indemnity, confidentiality, gross negligence/willful misconduct only) keep the cap effective.

## 2. INDEMNIFICATION MECHANICS

### 2.1 Master Services Agreement (Article VIII)

**Current:** Indemnify/defend/hold harmless; indemnified party to give notice, grant control, cooperate.

**Redlined replacement for 8.3:**

**8.3 Indemnification Procedures.** The indemnified Party shall (a) provide prompt written notice of any claim for which indemnification is sought (provided that delay shall not relieve the indemnifying Party except to the extent materially prejudiced); (b) grant the indemnifying Party **sole and exclusive** control over the defense and settlement of the claim; (c) not settle or compromise any such claim without the indemnifying Party's **prior written consent**; and (d) provide reasonable cooperation (at the indemnifying Party's expense) in the defense. The indemnifying Party shall not settle any claim in a manner that imposes non-monetary obligations on the indemnified Party or admits fault on behalf of the indemnified Party without the indemnified Party's prior written consent. **No indemnification shall apply to the extent a claim arises from the combination of the Deliverables (or Services) with materials, systems, or use not supplied or authorized by Service Provider, or from modification of the Deliverables by any party other than Service Provider, unless such combination or modification was approved or directed by Service Provider in writing.**

**Rationale:** Sole control of defense and settlement consent reduce procedural disputes; combination/modification carve-out limits liability for client or third-party changes.

---

### 2.2 Independent Contractor Agreement (Article VII)

**Add after 7.1:**

**7.2 Indemnification Procedures.** Company shall provide prompt written notice of any claim. Contractor shall have sole control of the defense and settlement of any claim; Company shall not settle or admit fault without Contractor's prior written consent and shall provide reasonable cooperation at Contractor's expense. Contractor's indemnification obligation shall not apply to the extent a claim arises from the combination of the Work Product with materials or use not supplied or authorized by Contractor, or from modification of the Work Product by Company or a third party.

**Rationale:** Aligns contractor indemnity procedures with MSA and adds combination/modification exclusion.

## 4. DATA PROCESSING ADDENDUM (DPA)

### 4.1 Audit and Subprocessor Liability (privacy_policy_dpa.md and TorpedoWeb LLC DPA)

**Redlined replacement for audit obligation (Part B §3.9 or DPA §6):**

**Audits.** Processor shall make available to Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and shall allow for and contribute to audits **no more than once per Contract Year**, conducted by Controller or an auditor mandated by Controller, **upon at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice**. **Audits shall be conducted remotely unless remote audit is not reasonably sufficient to verify compliance; any on-site audit shall be limited to one (1) per Contract Year and to Processor's normal business hours.** **Controller and any auditor shall keep all information and audit results strictly confidential and shall use them only to verify compliance and exercise Controller's rights under Data Protection Laws.** Controller shall bear the cost of audits. "Contract Year" means each twelve (12) month period during the term of the Service Agreement.

**Subprocessor liability (add to DPA):** **Processor remains liable to Controller for subprocessor performance to the same extent as under the Service Agreement, but in no event shall Processor's liability under this DPA (including for subprocessor acts or omissions) exceed the limitation of liability set forth in the Service Agreement.**

**Force majeure (add to DPA or MSA):** **Neither Party shall be liable for delay or failure to perform due to causes beyond its reasonable control (including acts of God, war, terrorism, pandemic, government action, or critical infrastructure failure). If such delay exceeds thirty (30) days, either Party may terminate the affected order upon written notice. Obligations to protect Personal Data and notify of breach shall continue during the force majeure period.**

**Rationale:** Audit frequency (once per year), remote-first and notice period reduce operational burden; confidentiality of audit results protects Processor; subprocessor liability tied to agreement cap avoids uncapped DPA exposure; force majeure timing enables exit and clarifies ongoing data obligations.

## 7. OPERATING AGREEMENT (6 Del. C. § 18-1101 and Manager Protections)

### 7.1 Fiduciary Duties, Exculpation, Advancement, No Duty to Present Opportunities

**Redlined new Article (insert after Article V or as new Article V-A):**

**ARTICLE V-A: LIMITATION OF DUTIES; EXCULPATION; ADVANCEMENT**

**5A.1 Limitation of Fiduciary Duties.** To the fullest extent permitted by the Delaware Limited Liability Company Act, 6 Del. C. § 18-1101(c), and any other applicable law, the doctrine of fiduciary duty shall not apply to the Manager(s) or Members. The duties of the Manager(s) and Members are limited to the contractual duties set forth in this Agreement. To the extent that any fiduciary duty is deemed to apply, such duty is hereby eliminated to the maximum extent permitted by 6 Del. C. § 18-1101(c).

**5A.2 Exculpation.** No Manager or Member shall be liable to the Company or any Member for any claim arising out of or in connection with the business or affairs of the Company, except for (a) acts or omissions involving bad faith, willful misconduct, or knowing violation of law, or (b) any transaction from which the Manager or Member derived an improper personal benefit. This exculpation is in addition to any limitation of liability permitted under the Act.

**5A.3 Advancement of Expenses.** The Company may, in the discretion of the Manager(s), advance reasonable expenses (including attorneys' fees) incurred by a Manager or Member in defending any proceeding in advance of final disposition, provided that the recipient furnishes an undertaking to repay if it is ultimately determined that the recipient is not entitled to indemnification. The right to advancement is contractual and not exclusive of any other right under the Act or this Agreement.

**5A.4 No Duty to Present Opportunities.** Neither the Manager(s) nor any Member shall have any duty to offer to the Company any business opportunity of which they become aware, and each may pursue such opportunities for their own account or for the account of others, unless such opportunity was developed using Company resources or Confidential Information in breach of this Agreement.

**Rationale:** 6 Del. C. § 18-1101(c) permits contractual limitation of fiduciary duties; exculpation and discretionary advancement protect managers/members from routine claims; no duty to present opportunities avoids conflict claims for outside opportunities.

## 8. ARBITRATION, JURY WAIVER, CLASS WAIVER, DELAWARE VENUE

### 8.1 Master Services Agreement (Article X)

**Redlined replacement for 10.2:**

**10.2 Dispute Resolution.** Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement or any SOW, or the breach, termination, or invalidity thereof, shall be resolved as follows. **THE PARTIES WAIVE ANY RIGHT TO A JURY TRIAL AND AGREE THAT ANY PROCEEDING SHALL BE CONDUCTED ON AN INDIVIDUAL BASIS AND NOT IN A CLASS, CONSOLIDATED, OR REPRESENTATIVE ACTION.** Such dispute shall be settled by binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association (AAA) in accordance with its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The place of arbitration shall be Wilmington, Delaware. The arbitration shall be conducted by a single arbitrator. **The arbitration and all related proceedings, including the existence, content, and outcome of the arbitration, shall be kept confidential by the Parties and the arbitrator except as required by law or to enforce an award.** The award rendered by the arbitrator shall be final and binding upon the Parties, and judgment thereon may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction in the State of Delaware. **The Parties consent to exclusive jurisdiction and venue in the state and federal courts located in Wilmington, Delaware for any action to enforce an award or for any proceeding not subject to arbitration.**

**Rationale:** Jury waiver and class/consolidated/representative waiver reduce litigation risk and cost; arbitration confidentiality protects business information; Delaware venue ensures consistency across the corpus.
