# INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR AGREEMENT

This Independent Contractor Agreement (this "Agreement") is made and entered into as of **[Effective Date]** ("Effective Date"), by and between:

**TorpedoWeb LLC**, a Delaware limited liability company, with its principal place of business at **[Company Address]** ("Company"),

and

**[Contractor Full Legal Name]**, an individual residing at **[Contractor Address]**, or a **[Entity Type]** with its principal place of business at **[Contractor Address]** ("Contractor").

Company and Contractor are hereinafter collectively referred to as the "Parties" and individually as a "Party."

---

## RECITALS

A. Company is engaged in the business of providing digital engineering services and requires the services of an independent contractor with specialized skills.

B. Contractor possesses the necessary skills, experience, and expertise to perform the services required by Company and desires to do so as an independent contractor.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## ARTICLE I: SERVICES

**1.1 Scope of Services.** Contractor shall perform the services described in **Exhibit A** or in any written **Statement of Work ("SOW")** agreed upon by the Parties (collectively, the "Services"). Each SOW shall be subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement and shall be incorporated by reference when executed by both Parties.

**1.2 Standard of Performance.** Contractor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, in accordance with industry standards, and in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations and with any Company policies communicated to Contractor in writing.

**1.3 Manner and Means; Independent Control.** Contractor retains sole discretion over the method, details, and means of performing the Services, subject to any specifications contained in the applicable SOW. Company shall not control the method or means by which Contractor performs the Services. Contractor shall determine the manner and means by which the Services are accomplished.

**1.4 Equipment.** Unless otherwise stated in the applicable SOW or in writing, Contractor shall supply all equipment, software, tools, and materials necessary to perform the Services at Contractor's own expense.

---

## ARTICLE II: TERM AND TERMINATION

**2.1 Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue until the Services under all SOWs are completed or until terminated earlier in accordance with this Article II.

**2.2 Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement for any reason upon **[10–30]** days' prior written notice to the other Party.

**2.3 Termination for Cause.**

(a) **By Company:** Company may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if Contractor: (i) materially breaches this Agreement or an SOW and fails to cure such breach within **[15]** days after receiving written notice thereof (or such longer period as may be reasonable under the circumstances); (ii) violates confidentiality obligations under Article VI; (iii) infringes or misappropriates intellectual property rights; or (iv) engages in misconduct, fraud, or illegal activity in connection with the Services or this Agreement.

(b) **By Either Party:** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches any provision of this Agreement or an SOW and fails to cure such breach within **[15]** days after receiving written notice thereof.

**2.4 Effect of Termination.** Upon termination:

(a) Company shall pay Contractor for all Services performed and all pre-approved expenses incurred up to the effective date of termination; all outstanding invoices for approved work shall become payable in accordance with the payment terms in Exhibit A or the applicable SOW.

(b) Contractor shall immediately cease representing himself or herself as affiliated with or acting on behalf of Company.

(c) Contractor shall immediately return to Company all Confidential Information and all Work Product (including all copies) in Contractor's possession or control, or permanently delete such materials where return is not practicable, and certify in writing that such return or deletion has been completed.

(d) The following Articles shall survive termination: **Articles III** (Compensation, for amounts owed), **IV** (Independent Contractor Status), **V** (Intellectual Property), **VI** (Confidentiality), **VII** (Indemnification), **VIII** (Restrictive Covenants), **IX** (Dispute Resolution), and **X** (Miscellaneous).

---

## ARTICLE III: COMPENSATION

**3.1 Fees.** Company shall pay Contractor the fees specified in Exhibit A or the applicable SOW, at the rate and according to the payment schedule set forth therein.

**3.2 Invoicing.** Contractor shall submit invoices as described in Exhibit A or the applicable SOW. Invoices shall be submitted in accordance with the frequency and format specified therein.

**3.3 Expenses.** Company shall reimburse Contractor for reasonable and **pre-approved** out-of-pocket expenses incurred in performing the Services only to the extent specified in Exhibit A or the applicable SOW. Contractor shall provide supporting documentation for any reimbursable expenses.

**3.4 Taxes.** Contractor is solely responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes arising from or related to the compensation paid under this Agreement, including income taxes, self-employment taxes, social contributions, and VAT/GST or similar obligations (if applicable). Company shall not withhold payroll taxes, and no amount paid hereunder shall be construed as salary or wages. Contractor shall provide Company with a completed IRS Form W-9 (for U.S. contractors) or W-8BEN/W-8BEN-E (or equivalent for international contractors) prior to commencing Services.

**3.5 Tax Indemnity.** Contractor shall indemnify and hold harmless Company from and against any and all claims, penalties, interest, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from any determination by a taxing or regulatory authority that Contractor is an employee of Company or that Company is responsible for withholding or remitting taxes with respect to Contractor's compensation. Contractor represents that Contractor is customarily engaged in an independently established trade, occupation, or business of the same nature as the Services and that this engagement is non-exclusive. Company makes no representation as to Contractor's classification, and Contractor has had the opportunity to seek independent tax and legal advice.

---

## ARTICLE IV: INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS

Contractor is an **independent contractor** and not an employee, partner, agent, or joint venturer of Company. Contractor shall not be entitled to any employee benefits, including but not limited to health insurance, retirement plans, paid time off, or workers' compensation. Contractor has no authority to enter into contracts or agreements on behalf of Company or to otherwise bind Company. Contractor shall not represent himself or herself as a Company employee or claim any employment benefits. Contractor may perform services for other clients; Company does not require Contractor to work exclusively for Company.

---

## ARTICLE V: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

**5.1 Work Made for Hire.** All deliverables, designs, software, code, systems, documentation, inventions, discoveries, improvements, data, algorithms, or other materials created, conceived, or developed by Contractor (either alone or with others) in the course of performing the Services under this Agreement or any SOW (collectively, "Work Product") shall be considered **"work made for hire"** to the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, and Company shall be the sole and exclusive owner of all right, title, and interest in and to the Work Product.

**5.2 Present Assignment.** To the extent any Work Product does not qualify as a work made for hire, Contractor **hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys to Company, on a present and automatic basis**, all worldwide right, title, and interest in and to the Work Product and all intellectual property rights therein, including copyrights, patent rights, trade secrets, database rights, design rights, all derivative works, and all rights to enforce or sue for infringement. This transfer occurs **automatically upon creation**, without the need for additional documentation.

**5.3 Moral Rights Waiver.** To the fullest extent permitted by applicable law, Contractor irrevocably waives all moral rights (including rights of attribution and integrity) in the Work Product. Contractor agrees that Company may modify, adapt, combine, commercialize, or remove attribution for the Work Product without restriction. Where moral rights cannot legally be waived, Contractor agrees **not to assert them against Company or its clients.**

**5.4 Contractor Background IP.** Contractor shall disclose any pre-existing intellectual property, software, tools, methodologies, or other materials developed or acquired by Contractor prior to or independently of this Agreement ("Background IP") that Contractor incorporates into the Work Product. Contractor retains ownership of Background IP. Contractor grants Company a **perpetual, worldwide, irrevocable, royalty-free, sublicensable** license to use, reproduce, modify, and distribute such Background IP solely as incorporated into the Work Product and as necessary for the operation and use of the Work Product.

**5.5 Further Assurances.** Contractor shall execute any documents and take any actions reasonably requested by Company, at Company's expense, to confirm Company's ownership, perfect or register intellectual property rights in the Work Product, and enforce IP protections.

**5.6 Power of Attorney.** If Contractor fails to execute any document required under this Article V within a reasonable time after request, Contractor appoints Company as **attorney-in-fact**, coupled with an interest, to execute such documents solely for the purpose of securing Company's intellectual property rights in the Work Product.

---

## ARTICLE VI: CONFIDENTIALITY

**6.1 Confidential Information.** "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by Company or obtained by Contractor in the course of performing the Services, including but not limited to: source code, product architecture, software systems, pricing, client lists, trade secrets, security systems, business strategy, technical documentation, and any other information designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential.

**6.2 Non-Disclosure Obligations.** Contractor agrees to: (a) maintain strict confidentiality of all Confidential Information; (b) use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing the Services for Company; and (c) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without Company's prior written consent, except as required by law (subject to the notice and cooperation provisions below).

**6.3 Security.** Contractor shall implement reasonable administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect Confidential Information from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.

**6.4 Return or Destruction.** Upon termination of this Agreement or at any time upon Company's request, Contractor shall immediately return or permanently delete all Confidential Information and all copies thereof and certify in writing that such return or deletion has been completed. Contractor may retain one copy solely for legal compliance or defense purposes, subject to continued confidentiality.

**6.5 International Contractors.** Where Contractor resides or operates outside the United States, Contractor consents to the enforcement of this Agreement under Delaware law, agrees that confidentiality obligations apply regardless of local jurisdiction, and acknowledges that trade secret and confidentiality protections are critical to Company.

**6.6 Legal Compelled Disclosure.** If Contractor is required by law to disclose Confidential Information, Contractor shall (to the extent permitted by law) give Company prompt written notice and cooperate with Company in seeking a protective order or similar relief. Contractor shall disclose only that portion of Confidential Information that is legally required.

**6.7 Injunctive Relief.** Contractor acknowledges that breach of this Article VI may cause Company irreparable harm for which monetary damages may be inadequate. Company may seek injunctive relief, specific performance, or other equitable relief to enforce this Article, without the requirement of posting a bond, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

---

## ARTICLE VII: INDEMNIFICATION

**7.1 Contractor Indemnification.** Contractor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Company and its affiliates, officers, directors, employees, agents, and clients from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to: (a) Contractor's breach of this Agreement or any SOW; (b) any claim that the Services or Work Product infringe or misappropriate any third-party intellectual property rights (except to the extent arising solely from materials expressly provided by Company to Contractor for incorporation); (c) unauthorized use of third-party materials or open-source license violations in connection with the Work Product; (d) failure to obtain assignments or licenses from subcontractors or others who contribute to the Work Product; (e) any act or omission of Contractor in performing the Services; or (f) any claim that Contractor is an employee of Company or that Company is responsible for any employment-related obligations with respect to Contractor.

**7.2 Indemnification Procedures.** Company shall provide Contractor with prompt written notice of any claim for which indemnification is sought. Contractor shall have sole control of the defense and settlement of any such claim; provided that Company shall not settle or admit fault without Contractor's prior written consent and shall provide reasonable cooperation at Contractor's reasonable expense. Contractor's indemnification obligation shall not apply to the extent a claim arises solely from the combination of the Work Product with materials or use not supplied or authorized by Contractor, or from modification of the Work Product by Company or a third party after delivery by Contractor.

**7.3 Payment.** Contractor shall pay all damages, settlements, and reasonable legal costs (including Company's reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from claims within the scope of Section 7.1.

---

## ARTICLE VIII: RESTRICTIVE COVENANTS

Contractor acknowledges that the restrictions in this Article VIII are reasonable and necessary to protect Company's legitimate business interests.

**8.1 Non-Solicitation of Clients.** During the term of this Agreement and for **12 (twelve) months** after termination, Contractor shall not directly or indirectly solicit business from, or provide substantially similar services to, any Company client for whom Contractor provided Services during the **12 (twelve) months** prior to termination, for the purpose of diverting or replacing the business relationship between Company and such client.

**8.2 Non-Solicitation of Personnel.** During the term of this Agreement and for **12 (twelve) months** after termination, Contractor shall not recruit, solicit, or induce any employee or contractor of Company to leave Company or to work for Contractor or any third party.

**8.3 Limited Non-Compete.** During the term of this Agreement, Contractor shall not provide substantially similar services to a direct competitor of Company where such services would involve the use or disclosure of Company Confidential Information. This restriction does **not** prohibit general freelance or consulting activity unrelated to Company's trade secrets or Confidential Information.

**8.4 Blue Pencil.** If any court of competent jurisdiction determines that any provision of this Article VIII is unenforceable because of its scope, duration, or geographic area, the Parties agree that the court may modify such provision to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable under applicable law (including Delaware law), and as so modified the provision shall remain in effect.

---

## ARTICLE IX: GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

**9.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflicts of laws principles.

**9.2 Arbitration.** THE PARTIES WAIVE ANY RIGHT TO A JURY TRIAL AND AGREE THAT ANY PROCEEDING SHALL BE CONDUCTED ON AN INDIVIDUAL BASIS AND NOT IN A CLASS, CONSOLIDATED, OR REPRESENTATIVE ACTION. Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement, or the breach, termination, or invalidity thereof, shall be settled by **binding arbitration** administered by the American Arbitration Association ("AAA") in accordance with its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The place of arbitration shall be **Wilmington, Delaware**. The arbitration shall be conducted by a single arbitrator. The arbitration and all related proceedings shall be kept confidential by the Parties and the arbitrator except as required by law or to enforce an award. The award rendered by the arbitrator shall be final and binding upon the Parties, and judgment thereon may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction in the State of Delaware. The Parties consent to exclusive jurisdiction and venue in the state and federal courts located in Wilmington, Delaware for any action to enforce an award or for any proceeding not subject to arbitration.

---

## ARTICLE X: MISCELLANEOUS

**10.1 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with Exhibit A and any SOWs, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions, whether oral or written.

**10.2 Amendments.** No amendment or modification of this Agreement or any SOW shall be effective unless in writing and signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

**10.3 Assignment.** Contractor may not assign this Agreement or any rights or obligations hereunder without the prior written consent of Company. Any attempted assignment in violation of this section shall be void. Company may assign this Agreement in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets.

**10.4 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

**10.5 Waiver.** No waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by the Party against whom the waiver is sought. A waiver of any breach shall not constitute a waiver of any subsequent breach.

**10.6 Electronic Signatures.** This Agreement and any SOWs may be executed by electronic signature, which shall be considered as an original signature for all purposes.

**10.7 Non-Exclusivity.** Contractor may perform services for other clients; Company does not require Contractor to work exclusively for Company.

**10.8 Waiver of Equitable Accounting.** Contractor waives any right to an equitable accounting or to inspect Company's books and records in connection with the Services or this Agreement, except as required by law.

**10.9 Notices.** Any notice required or permitted under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by email (with confirmation of receipt), certified mail, or nationally recognized courier to the addresses set forth above (or such other address as a Party may specify in writing). Notices shall be deemed given when received.
